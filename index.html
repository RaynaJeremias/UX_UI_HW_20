Government Website
Redesign
Case Study

Rayna Jeremias & Talia Dobrec
UX/UI Designers

Project Duration- 2 weeks


Overview
Project Brief-
Redesign a government website based on a comprehensive
user analysis and heuristics evaluation.
The Problem-
Arizona.gov needed a major overhaul, from content to continuity.
The Goal-
To create an esthetically pleasing site that has excellent usability.

  • Research
  • Ideation
  • Design
  • Testing



Research

My partner and I began our project by doing a heuristic evaluation of the current website. We created a short user test and had people try to navigate the site. The website felt cold and unwelcoming, with no content other than a single, large image of Coronavirus. Navigation had many flaws, including clicking the tab you wanted and landing on another option page, which was frustrating to users. Another issue was the overwhelming amount of external links.

Pain Points
•Navigation confusion
•Overwhelming amount of external links
•Not a lot of content

My partner used Google Analytics to find key data on our site.
It was surprising to see how high the site bounce rate was- 56%




Ideation


We created a typical user and gave her a background story to imagine why she would use this site.

Doing this helped us find key problems in the navigation of the site.



Design

Because we had such a short turnaround, we really dove right in to mid-fi design. We played around with a few different layouts until we found the right one. We focused on the details of making the site beautiful and easy to use.



Testing

The first round of testing showed usability issues such as- difficulty clicking buttons, trouble navigating back, trying to click on non-clickable areas. The second round of testing showed that users were still having issues clicking buttons.

Findings
•Enlarge dropdown icons
•Make clickable areas bigger
•Add back button

Conclusion

Going foreward, my partner and I plan on iterating the design of our site, changing the secondary navigation, and updating the logo. From there, we would like to code the site to add more user interaction capabilities.
